# ACT 1 Beat Sheet (The starting point & the incident)

**Opening Image:** The Island of Alosaid. Showing Vane, cold and relentless, killing the Beast-Man (Symon Eirth) by 
"glitching" him and then inadvertently trampling the flower, tells the player everything they need to 
know: Vane is a lethal machine of the Empire, the natural world is beautiful but suppressed, and within 
Vane there is a tiny, suppressed crack of humanity.

**Theme Stated:** An Empire radio transmission presents the universe as matter to decode, bend, and correct,
expressing the human doctrine of imposed form that the story will later dismantle.

**Set Up:** A playable tutorial returns to Vane’s adolescence and military training, teaching the player 
the fundamentals of combat and alchemy while revealing the Empire of Antimur, one of the three 
surviving human empires, and the machinery that shaped Vane into an elite Executioner selected for a 
covert mission on Alosaid.

**Catalyst:** Returning from the flashback, Vane activates the mutation that steals Symon’s body and 
identity, severing his stable connection to the Empire and trapping him inside a life he does not understand. 
Forced to improvise his way through Symon’s home, he hallucinates/raves when confronted with the wife 
and children of the man he erased and destroys them, because he justify this moment of complete inhumanity 
as "erasing" some echoes of some monsters turning the infiltration into an irreparable moral crime, and 
making this the first of many psychological ghosts for Vane.

**Debate:** Standing over the bodies, Vane’s HUD fully reboots, dropping the "anomaly" filter to reveal the 
human corpses of Symon's family. Suddenly, his radio crackles. Imperial Command demands a sitrep, having 
detected a massive spike in Vane's stress biomarkers and an unsanctioned weapon discharge. Command asks: 
"Executioner, report. Did you encounter hostiles?"
The game gives the player a grim objective: Hide the evidence. The player must physically drag the bodies 
into the house's basement furnace to avoid failing the stealth infiltration. Vane's breathing is ragged,
his hands shaking (represented by aim-sway). Standing before the roaring fire, command repeats the question,
threatening to abort the mission. Vane physically injects his neck with a chemical stabilizer to force his 
heart rate down. He presses the comms button and lies: "Negative, Command. Minor biological anomalies. 
Purged. Infiltration secure." To finalize the choice, he rips Symon’s work coat off a hook, puts it over 
his bloodied armor, and steps out into the streets, leaving the ashes of his victims behind.

**Break Into Two:** After destroying what remained of Symon's life, Vane chooses not to interrupt the 
mission and ventures into the interior of Alosaid under the identity of the man he erased, entering a 
world that he can no longer observe merely as a target of the Empire. Therefore he goes to the Wooden city,
after seeing a sign in a road near the ex-house of Symon.


# ACT 2 Beat Sheet

## Phase A (The Exploration & The Promise of the Premise)

**B Story:** Vane trails Lyra through the Wooden City, copying her footsteps to bypass the shifting streets 
and deadly traps. Bleeding heavily after an archive raid, Vane hides in Lyra's home; as she bandages 
his wounds, he drops his disguise and holds her hand. A moment of genuine, gentle connection occurs—something 
Vane has never felt. Terrified by his own humanity, Vane convinces himself that Symon's memories are 
taking over his brain. To bury these "rogue emotions," Vane manually activates his suit's emotion suppression 
system, physically shocking himself until his Imperial conditioning resets. For the rest of the mission, 
Vane hallucinates Lyra's voice in his audio static and sees her face in the shadows, causing him to 
freeze during combat.

**Fun and Games:** Vane infiltrates the Alchemist's Archive by dissolving through a ventilation grate,
stealing three vials of "living ink" that rewrite matter on contact. He tests one on a locked door—
watching it bloom into wood, then bone, then rust—before the second vial accidentally cracks in his palm,
fusing his glove to the wall. He tries to stop the mutation using the 255-octagram alchemy, leaving 
the hand exposed but structurally altered. Now, his bare hand involuntarily tries to "read and rewrite"
the property of objects it touches. Vane is forced to bandage and hide it, a physical manifestation 
of his failing Imperial form. He scales the Cathedral of Hours using magnetic pitons, bypassing the 
singing guardians by timing his movements to their harmonic intervals. At the apex, he plants a signal 
beacon disguised as a weather vane, but the wind shifts; the beacon's hum attracts a swarm of glass 
moths that shred his cloak and carve hairline fractures into his visor. He smashes them with an EMP 
pulse, blacks out the district, and rappels into the blackout chaos below. In the Sunken Market, 
Vane barters Symon's identity for forged transit papers, but the forger recognizes the face from a 
missing person's broadside. Vane breaks the man's fingers before he can shout, takes the papers and 
the man's ring as collateral, and escapes through a fishmonger's tunnel choked with eel-silk. 
He emerges stinking of brine, the ring cutting into his palm, and realizes he no longer remembers 
which pocket holds his Imperial insignia.

**Midpoint:** Vane locates the dormant Titan buried beneath the Wooden City and discovers Lyra already 
there, chanting the awakening rite. She turns, looking at his true face, not Symon's. She has known since 
the bandages. Instead of screaming, she offers him a choice: help her wake the Titan to shatter the Empire's 
hold, or shoot her and complete the mission. Vane raises his weapon, trembling. His conditioning screams 
at him to fire, but he can't. He lowers the gun, sparing her.
Confused and desperate, Vane bursts into the Sanctum alone to find the Source-Heart. It is not a weapon,
but a child—a human infant sustained by the island’s living matter, the pure human ultum born centuries 
ago. The Empire’s true mission is extraction. Vane’s radio crackles: “Retrieve the vessel. Terminate 
the host organism.” Vane hesitates. Ready to execute the Child, Vane locks eyes with him and is 
catapulted into a mystical vision. There, he encounters the adult avatar of the Child, discovering to his horror 
that he is merely an artificial clone created from his divine DNA. Struck by this spiritual awakening,
Vane returns to reality, breaks free from his imperial conditioning, and spares the Child. His entire 
existence is a lie. Vane fires on the support team, destroying his extraction 
beacon. The island screams. Alosaid awakens.

## Phase B (The Consequences & The Fall)

**Bad Guys Close In:** Following Vane’s rebellion, the Empire deploys a "Sanitizer" to erase the anomaly:
Preceptor Oram, a towering zealot clad in a blindingly bright yellow bio-suit, riding a screeching, magnetic 
monowheel. Contrasting the island's organic grunge, Oram wields "Sky-Seraphs"—geometric, hard-light drones 
that sing perfect, agonizing hymns (like a choir of mechanical spirits) as they vaporize Alosaid's awakened 
wildlife. Oram ambushes Vane and Lyra on the collapsing bridges of the Wooden City. He doesn't hide in 
the shadows; he attacks in broad daylight, broadcasting a cheerful, terrifying sermon about "correcting 
the equation." During the frantic boss fight, Oram's precise light-whips slash through Vane's stolen 
coat and overload his mutation matrix, physically burning away Symon's face and exposing Vane's true, 
scarred features, along with his wildly mutating ink-hand, to Lyra. Forced into a corner, Vane uses his 
corrupted ink-hand to infect Oram's pristine drones, causing a catastrophic explosion that shatters the 
bridge. Oram retreats, laughing, while Vane falls into the abyss of the island's roots, separated from 
Lyra and the "Child".

**All Is Lost:** Plunging into the sunless root-caverns of Alosaid, Vane’s Imperial armor finally dies,
leaving him suffocating in the dark. Stripped of his HUD and identity, he physically rips the dead 
mechanical plating from his chest and forces his mutating ink-hand to emit a bioluminescent glow. Guided 
by the ink's pulse, he scavenges glowing pages of the ancient "Book of Mutations" clutched in the roots.
Instead of reading them, Vane presses the heavy parchment directly into his infected palm; the living 
ink dissolves the paper, absorbing the chaotic alchemical knowledge straight into his bloodstream and 
violently rewriting his nervous system.

**Dark Night of the Soul:** As the alchemical knowledge ravages his nervous system, Vane experiences 
a grueling, playable hallucination. His Imperial HUD desperately tries to reboot, flashing red and classifying 
his own beating heart as a "Fatal Anomaly." He is hunted by the digital ghosts of Symon's family and 
his own clone-face. Vane plants the seeds of Alosaid flowers on the ashes of Symon and his family. 
Reaching the breaking point, Vane refuses to shock himself. Instead, he physically 
gags and vomits out the cybernetic stabilizer he injected in Act 1, purging the Empire from his body. 
Kneeling in the bioluminescent dirt, he finds Lyra’s torn scarf, dropped during her capture by Oram. 
As his monstrous, mutated ink-hand picks it up, it doesn't destroy or rewrite the fabric; it gently protects 
it. Vane realizes the tenderness he felt wasn't a glitch or Symon's ghost, it is his own. He is human, and he loves her.

**Break Into Three:** Embracing his humanity and his terrifying new biological form, Vane makes a conscious 
choice: the mission is dead, the Empire is dead, he is going to save Lyra and protect the origin of his 
life. He slams his mutating hand into the cavern floor and, fully utilizing the Book's knowledge, commands 
the island's colossal roots to violently tunnel through the deep earth. Riding the massive, growing wooden 
tendril, Vane crashes through the ancient bedrock, bursting directly into the Sanctum—the Titan's cradle.
His root-ram violently shatters the pristine, geometric Extraction Rig that Preceptor Oram had built around the Child.

# ACT 3 Beat Sheet (The Synthesis)

**Finale:** The "Save the Princess" assault in the heart of Alosaid. Oram has chained Lyra to the Empire's
rig, extracting her alchemical blood to bypass the Child's organic defenses. Vane storms the Sanctum 
not as a stealth executioner, but as a chaotic force of nature, using his ink-hand to physically rewrite 
the Empire's perfect metal containment shields into brittle glass. During the final boss fight, Vane 
ignores Oram's perfect light-drones. He charges through the pain, physically grappling Oram and using 
the ink-hand to rewrite Oram's pristine yellow bio-suit into a coffin of suffocating, thorny roots. Vane 
rips Lyra free from the machine. However, the automated extraction sequence is still draining the Child.
To stop it, Vane jams his own mutating arm into the Empire's biometric terminal and injects his own 
genetically identical clone-blood. This creates a massive "System Paradox" that crashes the Imperial 
network and triggers the Sanctum to seal itself inside an impenetrable, blooming shell of living wood, safe forever.

**Final Image:** Vane and Lyra stand on the shore of a fully awakened Alosaid. Vane is stripped of his 
armor, heavily scarred, his arm permanently mutated into living wood and ink—but his face is finally 
his own, and relaxed. He looks down at a native glowing flower. This time, instead of mechanically glitching 
and trampling it (like in the Opening Image), his monstrous hand gently strokes the petals.
