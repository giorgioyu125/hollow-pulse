# ACTS SUMMARY

## Act 1
Vane Gauge arrives on the Island of Alosaid as an Executioner of the Human Empire to infiltrate the ranks 
of the Beast-men, which requires him to hide and disguise himself as a Beast-man. To do so, he takes the 
appearance of a Boar-Man passing through the port, whose name we will discover to be Symon Eirth, with a 
wife and two children whom Vane himself initially believes to be monsters, so he eliminates them as well 
when he finds them. But this episode, which at first causes him no complications, will haunt him when he 
realizes in Act 2B that he has simply killed innocent people.

## Act 2A
Under the guise of Symon Eirth, Vane crosses Alosaid and infiltrates sanctuaries, isolated masters, and 
living territories, expecting to find monsters and instead finding a civilization that appears decadent 
but is real. While learning forms of mutation that Imperial alchemy cannot read, his mission continues to 
crack under the weight of the lie and the identity he has stolen. Thus, between new alchemy and new 
discoveries, Vane commits many crimes and immoral acts.

## Act 2B
When the truth about the Island and the Book of Mutations emerges with clarity, Vane ceases to be a mere 
agent of the Empire and becomes a threat to the order he served. Hunted by his superiors and forced to 
choose how to use the change, he crosses the deepest and most irreversible part of his crisis.

## Act 3
Vane reaches the heart of the conflict between human imposition and natural mutation, where the fate of 
the Island coincides with the redefinition of his identity. The final clash with the Empire, with the 
Ancestral Truth, and with the very meaning of the Alchemy of Change decides what form the world can take 
after him.
