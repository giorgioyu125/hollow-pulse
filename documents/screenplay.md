# HOLLOW PULSE - Gameplay Script - First Draft - 30 March 2026

**FADE IN:**

**TITLE CARD: The Island of Alosaid. The Outer Reaches.**

### **[SCENE 1]**

**EXT. Port of Alosaid, 1:00 AM**

A man with a boar-like face is sitting on a small wooden bench at the pier, fishing, with a bucket beside 
him. By accident, this man drops his wallet on the ground, and just as he picks it up, a boat crashing 
into the pier knocks him off his chair. Frightened, the man asks whoever is steering the boat:

                                       SYMON EIRTH (On-Screen)
              Who are you?! Can you tell me why you had to make all that noise?!

But as soon as he finishes speaking, we see another man, Vane the protagonist, who with a clean swing 
of his axe severs the man’s neck, killing him. But the moment he lands the blow, the man’s body and his 
image distort, and Vane immediately places his hand on the body and absorbs the image, which merges with 
his own to create a unique blend. Then heads home with his fishing rod; a spotlight illuminates the 
pier, and as he leaves, he steps on a flower and squashes it into the ground.


### **[SCENE 2]**

**EXT. PORT STREET - CONTINUOUS**

Vane walks down a narrow alleyway of crooked, wooden houses. A rough, barely discernible path winding 
through the woods of Alosaid. He wears Symon’s thick fisherman’s coat over his dark, metallic armor. 
The coat drips seawater onto the cobblestones. An old, rusted loudspeaker mounted on a brick wall crackles to life.
A SLOW, HAUNTING VIOLIN MELODY begins to play Bach’s Partita No. 2 in D Minor. But the audio is heavily 
distorted, chopped apart by harsh digital static, turning a beautiful piece of music into something cold and oppressive. 
Over the music, a synthetic, calm Imperial voice broadcasts.

                                       IMPERIAL VOICE (Voice-Over)
    Matter is not chaos. Matter is an uncut gem. It awaits the Empire's chisel to decode, bend, and correct 
    its form. Compliance is geometry. Resistance is an error. 

Vane ignores the broadcast. He stops under a flickering gas lamp. He opens Symon’s wallet. Inside: a
faded photograph of a woman holding a toddler, and a transit pass stamped with an address: 14 Mulberry Lane.

Vane looks at the photo. His expression doesn't change. He pockets the wallet and keeps walking. 
As his heavy boots step over a puddle, the ripples reflect his face—but for a split second, the reflection shows Symon’s beast-like features entirely, before snapping back to Vane's scarred visage.

### **[SCENE 3]**

**EXT. PORT DISTRICT - ALLEYWAYS - CONTINUOUS**

**[GAMEPLAY]** — STEALTH TRAVERSAL

**OBJECTIVE:** Reach the residential district. Avoid Imperial patrol drones.

Vane moves through a maze of trees and bushes between rusted shipping containers and old trees made of resin or glass.
Vane sees and passes near a lot of strange vegetation and sees a lot of alchmicalish creature, but one above all 
the most beautiful, it's a small fern made almost entirely of frozen mercury.
The player must crouch behind cover and time their movement to avoid the sweeping searchlights of two 
small, hovering patrol drones. The drones emit a soft, mechanical hum. If spotted, the player must quickly 
hide inside a container until the drones pass.

The alley opens into a small courtyard. A patrol drone is scanning the area. The player can choose to 
sneak past it or use Vane's axe to silently destroy it by throwing it at the drone's light sensor. Either 
way, beyond the courtyard, the path forks.

**PATH A:** A dark tunnel beneath a crumbling stone archway. Faster, but flooded with ankle-deep water that slows movement.

**PATH B:** A climb up a stack of containers and across a rope bridge. Exposes Vane to the open sky and a second searchlight, but keeps him dry.

Both paths converge at the edge of the residential district.

### **[SCENE 4]**

**EXT. THE GREAT FOUNDRY - ANTIMUR - 10 YEARS AGO**

The sky is a suffocating ceiling of smog and ash. No sun, no organic green—only jagged, towering spires 
of black iron and blinding yellow floodlights. 

This is the beating heart of Antimur, an Empire built on strict geometric perfection. 

**TITLE CARD: 10 YEARS AGO - THE GREAT FOUNDRY, ANTIMUR**

We pan down to a massive, circular sparring pit etched with rigid, glowing mathematical grids. 

Standing in the center is VANE (Just 18 years old). He is leaner, his eyes less hollow but terrified, darting around.
He wears the gray, unadorned fatigues of an Initiate. In his hands, he tightly grips two standard-issue training axes.

High above, standing behind a thick pane of observation glass, is OVERSEER KORV. His voice booms through 
a massive, distorted PA system, echoing across the sterile courtyard.

                                       OVERSEER KORV (V.O.)
             The natural world is a chaotic variable. It rots. It mutates. It disobeys. 

A heavy, mechanized gate grinds open across the pit. A TRAINING SERVITOR steps out—a hulking, faceless automaton made of pristine steel, wielding a heavy broadsword.

                                      OVERSEER KORV (V.O.)
         We are the Empire of Antimur. We do not adapt to the world. We impose the form. 
                               Initiate Vane. Prove your geometry.

**[GAMEPLAY] - TUTORIAL STARTS - THE HALLS OF THE FOUNDRY**
**Mechanics to present:** Dodging, parrying, Octagrams powers, attacking and stress suppression.
**255 Octagram powers:** The Octagram 255 have three powers: Enchanced Parry, Matter Lock (Enchanced 
armor), Fossilize.

**[GAMEPLAY] - MINI BOSS FIGHT**
Here Vane will simply fight a Calcified Overseer deep within the bowels of the Great Foundry. Suspended 
over an impossibly deep, vertical abyss of interlocking iron teeth, the Foundry defies architectural 
logic—brass pipes that pump liquid Octagram matter snake across the smog-choked air in defiance of gravity,
connecting to massive, floating forges that echo with the deafening, rhythmic booming of colossal hammers.
It is a mechanical digestive tract designed to consume the earth and excrete Imperial order. Emerging 
from the steam and blinding magnesium flares is the Overseer: a hulking, vaguely humanoid mass of hyper-dense
slag and crystallized metal, its former human shape permanently fused into a jagged, walking furnace.
Dragging a massive, glowing forging press, the Overseer attacks with terrifying, mechanical weight; 
if Vane attempts to passively block its strikes, the sheer kinetic density of the slag-arms will instantly 
shatter his guard and crush him.

### **[SCENE 5]**

**JUMP CUT TO THE PRESENT:**

**EXT. 14 MULBERRY LANE - AFTER THE FLASHBACK**

A modest, two-story wooden house. Warm, amber light bleeds through the thin curtains of a ground-floor window. 
A faint smell of cooking fish hangs in the air.

Vane stands in the shadows across the street. He looks up at the window. 
He reaches to his belt and pulls out a small, glass vial filled with a black, shifting liquid—living ink. He readies it, expecting a hostile perimeter.

He picks up a axe. He moves toward the front door, his steps completely silent on the wet grass. 

### **[SCENE 6]**

**INT. 14 MULBERRY LANE - SYMON'S HOUSE - CONTINUOUS**

Vane pushes the door open. It creaks. 
Inside, the air is thick with heat from a cast-iron stove. 

A WOMAN stands at a wooden counter, her back to the door. She is humming a lullaby—the exact same melody 
as the distorted Bach from the streets, but pure and human. 

Next to the stove, a TODDLER sits on the floor, playing with a carved wooden boat. Vane stops three feet inside. 
A faint, translucent blue grid flashes over his field of vision—his Imperial HUD. 

The grid targets the Woman and the Child. 
Instead of showing human silhouettes, the HUD glitches violently. Red text flashes over their bodies: 
"*ANOMALY DETECTED. ECHO RECALLIBRATING.*"

Vane’s breathing becomes erratic. He grips the dagger tighter. His hand trembles, not with fear, but with a violent short-circuit in his brain. 

The Woman turns. She sees Symon’s face, but she freezes. She looks down at Vane’s bloodied boots, then up to the dark, metallic outline of his armor beneath the coat.

                                              WOMAN
                                   Symon? What happened to your—

Vane moves.
It is not a calculated military strike. It is a frantic, mechanical erasure. 

He crosses the distance in a blur. He clamps a heavy, armored hand over her mouth, muffling her scream. 
With the other hand, he drives the dagger upward into her chest. 

The Toddler screams. The high-pitched cry fills the small room.

Vane twists the blade. The Woman goes limp. He lowers her body to the floor with mechanical precision, making zero sound.

The Toddler is still screaming, crawling frantically toward the kitchen table. 

Vane stands up. His HUD is flashing wildly now, spitting red error codes across his vision. The static in his ears is deafening. 

He steps over the Woman’s body. He reaches under the table, grabs the Toddler by the ankle, and pulls 
him back into the light. Vane raises his boot. The Toddler looks up. Vane brings his heel down. The screaming stops. 
The only sound left in the room is the hissing of the cast-iron stove and the faint, relentless ticking of a wall clock. 

Vane stands perfectly still. His chest heaves. The blue HUD flickers, stabilizes, and turns green. 
Text appears: *ANOMALIES PURGED. INFILTRATION PROTOCOL SECURE.*

Vane looks down at the small, broken body on the floor. He wipes the dagger on Symon’s coat. 
Suddenly, a sharp BEEP emits from a communicator on his wrist. 

                                       IMPERIAL COMMAND (V.O.)
                   Executioner, report. We registered a massive spike in your stress 
               biomarkers and an unsanctioned weapon discharge. Did you encounter hostiles?

Vane stares at the blood pooling on the wooden floorboards. His hands are shaking violently.
After a few seconds of struggling to respond, he raises his hand and speaks into the radio.

                                               VANE
           Yeah, I ran into three Beast-men who threatened my identity. I had to take them out.
                                Everything's fine now; no one found out.

                                        IMPERIAL COMMAND (V.O.)
                Acknowledged. Proceed to primary objective. Avoid unnecessary contamination. 

The comm clicks dead. Vane exhales slowly through his nose. Vane physically injects his neck with a chemical stabilizer to force his heart rate down. And puts the used syringe in a bag.

He looks down at Symon's coat—soaked through at the front. He scans the room. On a hook by the door hangs 
a second, clean work coat. Vane strips the bloody coat off, lets it fall on the Woman's body, and pulls 
the clean one over his armor. He adjusts the collar and the radio comm on the wrist, that looks like 
a watch. He looks almost like a normal man again.

Then he looks back at the bodies. The blood is spreading toward the rug. It will seep through the floorboards.
Someone will see. Vane kneels down. He grabs the Woman by the wrists.

**[GAMEPLAY]** — BODY DISPOSAL

**OBJECTIVE:** Conceal the evidence. Drag both bodies to the basement furnace before the blood trail is noticed.

**OST:** Preludio n. 4 in Mi minore Chopin

**CONTENT:** The tone of this scene is bleak and desperate; we see the impassive protagonist dragging the bodies he 
has just killed, one by one. His gaze is empty and lost in the dark shroud of the night. His breathing 
is calm and steady, with the Empire's radio playing the soundtrack directly into his ears via his radio-watch 
to keep him calm. The player must spot the yellow dot on the fireplace, and then pick up and drag 
the bodies through the house, throwing them into the hot fireplace; they must wait for them to incinerate,
remove the ashes, and bury them in the garden. Vane's gaze as he watches the fire burn is steeped in 
the image reflected in his eyes. It turns 2:50 AM, so Vane goes to sleep and leaves everything as is.

use of a game mechanic called QUESTBOOK, in which the player checks what to do.
